This lesson will cover some new uses for many of the Photopea tools we have been working with as well as introduce you to some new, very useful tools. We will be working with the headshot photos we took in class to build a new you. You will use the faces of five famous people to build a new face for yourself. When finished, you should have an interesting image that looks somewhat natural, but mostly freaky. Smile, this is fun.

A word of caution: as you have already worked through the Student Astronaut (Space Scene) project, these instructions will not cover every single step and tell you how to do every single thing, though it will discuss the new techniques in more detail. Remember that if these instructions refer to something but do not discuss it in depth, that means that you should have already learned it. If you didn't learn it, it is your responsibility to refer back to the Space Scene tutorial, or hit the Photopea help section to figure it out. If those fail, work with another student to to solve your problem. If all else fails, ask your teacher...but they are your last resorts (take some pride in your work and attempt to solve your own problems - you will enjoy this process much more if you are doing the work yourself).

Before we begin, since you will be saving 5+ pictures of other people, please make a folder where you save your Photopea projects called: A New Me. It will be easier to locate your materials if they are all in one place.

  1. Launch Photopea and open your headshot (we used it in Space Scene)
  2. Choose: File/Save As PSD (in your A New Me folder)
  3. Name the file: ANewMe_name.psd (replacing YOUR name for the word NAME)

We are going to pull pieces off of other people's heads and use those pieces to replace various parts of your head. Take some time to look through the photos of all of the other people and choose five that you can use. You will be replacing the following five facial features: eyes, hair, ears, mouth, and nose...

4. Click here to view the headshot photos and when you find one, right-mouse click the picture, Save As, and save it into the folder in which you save all of your Photopea projects

For these directions, I have chosen five faces at random, and assigned facial parts to transfer to my original face. For your image, you can use whoever you wish, but you must replace the five facial features indicated below. Keep the following in mind as you choose replacements for each part:

  • Eyes

    • This includes the eye itself as well as the eye lashes and the area around it - you MUST use more than just the iris (the colored part), as it is easy to change a person's iris without it even looking like a change was made (and you don't even need another person to do it, you can just change the Hue/Saturation)
    • We want to reshape the entire eye, and not just replacement the eyeball itself

  • Hair

    • If you have lots of hair it will be difficult to remove it all and replace it with someone else's, so consider retaking your picture with your hair pulled back

  • Ears

    • Be sure the people you choose to use do not have hair covering their ears (notice that in three of the six photos above the ears are unusable)
    • If your picture has hair covering your ears, you will simply need to add in someone else's ears and place them over your hair (in other words, you don't get to skip this part because your ears are hard to see - you still have to give yourself new ears)

  • Mouth

    •  Choose a mouth that is of approximately the same size to your own, or one that can be easily scaled without looking silly (in other words, if you have a huge head don't choose a person with a tiny mouth)

  • Nose

    • Make sure your nose choice can be easily blended into the surrounding skin

Try to choose people with facial features different enough from yours that it actually makes a difference to your face. When finished, we should end up with an entirely different face...

Using this method, we have all types of image possibilities, even the really freaky and unnatural...

And we can even make an amazingly handsome and dashing man into something hideous...

LET'S GET STARTED!!

The first facial feature that we will replace is the eyes. You are free to use any of the images from the photo page that you want - when you see someone whose eyes you like, right mouse the picture and save it in your A New Me Folder that you created at the start of this step. When you save, name the picture with the body part (eye, nose, mouth...). Please do not look up other pictures on the Internet or spend too much time browsing these photos - just pick something and move on.
